## Design Context

### Users

Relay is for individual developers managing local software repositories. They use it during focused implementation work to organize plans, inspect repository state, preserve context, and complete development workflows without leaving the desktop application.

### Brand Personality

Dense, precise, and native. Relay should feel capable and trustworthy without becoming overwhelming, with the directness and information density of a polished desktop developer tool.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use Relay's light-mode native-utility direction: restrained surfaces, compact controls, clear hierarchy, strong keyboard usability, and readable technical detail. GitHub Desktop, Fork, and Tower are usability references; the result should remain visually consistent with Relay rather than imitate their branding. Avoid decorative website framing, fabricated information, excessive card nesting, and novelty that slows expert use.

### Design Principles

1. Reveal useful structure progressively so common actions stay simple and advanced detail remains close at hand.
2. Favor scanability, precise states, and compact hierarchy over decorative whitespace.
3. Make consequential Git actions explicit, reviewable, and supported by command-level evidence.
4. Preserve keyboard, screen-reader, reduced-motion, and narrow-window alternatives.
5. Keep the workbench extensible, while excluding destructive controls until their safety model is complete.
